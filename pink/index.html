Does the Color Pink really Exist?

In a blog post, Robert Krulwich of the public radio show Radiolab pointed out that the rainbow's hues do not include pink.

A FACT....

Remember the ROYGBIV mnemonic for the rainbow colors? The R(red) is the furthest thing from V(violet). That is the source of the problem. Pink can't exist in nature without a little rainbow-bending magic, which allows red and violet tones to mix. This has led experts to assume that “pink is a made-up color,” as Krulwich puts it.

That's all there is to it. Pink, the hue, is simply our brain's wishful thinking combining red and violet wavelengths to form the color of many little girls' birthday celebrations.
